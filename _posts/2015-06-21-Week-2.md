Well, another week done! How quick time flies when you are having fun! This
week we finished up "Collatz" and begun our next project "Netflix." I really
enjoyed working on Collatz because it helped me understand the inner-workings
of Python, which I have truly come to love. I was skeptical at first about
using the language, but it has really taken be my surprise with all of its
wonderful tools. One aspect I particularly like is its use of generators. To be
able to work with such large collections of data and not take up an ounce of
space until you need something is such an awesome benefit to using the
language. 

As far as the lectures go, I am getting my ass kicked by the quizzes, which
sucks because I study quite a bit for them. However, they are great practice
questions for the midterm and really do help me solidify the concepts.

On a side note, I recently find myself saying "god damn it" more often than
usual. :)

### Tip of The Week

Over the past couple of years I have really fell in love with the power of vim.
At first I did not quite understand what the big deal was about it, but once I
took the time to learn it, I have come to find it the most powerful text
editor.  So my tip this week is more so a recommendation to check it out via
this [tutorial](http://www.openvim.com/). What is great about vim too is that a
plugin exists for it within every IDE and even Sublime.
